Biography

Manoshi Bhuiyan is a Researcher at GeoScape Lab. She is currently pursuing her Master of Science in Climate Change and Development (CCD) at Independent University, Bangladesh (IUB) as an awardee of the COLOCAL Scholarship. Her academic foundation includes a Bachelor of Science (Hons.) in Environmental Science and Resource Management from Mawlana Bhashani Science and Technology University (MBSTU), where she graduated as top of her class. Her research focus centres on nature-based solutions, phytoremediation, and coastal adaptation strategies to support climate-affected communities.

Currently, she serves as an Executive Committee Member of the Institute of Climate Change, Environment and Sustainability (ICCES). Her career history includes professional roles such as a Research Assistant for a postdoctoral fellow of MIT and a Research Consultant at CEGIS, specialising in Land Use Land Cover (LULC) mapping and remote sensing technology. Additionally, she has contributed to international climate policy discourse, receiving recognition as a top presenter in global negotiations and climate finance training programmes.
