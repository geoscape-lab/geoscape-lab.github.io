Biography

Md. Zahid Hasan is an urban planning researcher and geospatial analyst based in Khulna, whose work centres on the application of Geographic Information Systems (GIS), Remote Sensing (RS), and data-driven methods to address urban and environmental challenges. His research primarily engages with themes of urban climate resilience, flood risk modelling, land use and land cover (LULC) dynamics, and spatial accessibility of urban services, with a strong emphasis on methodological rigour and policy relevance in developing-country contexts.

His publication record reflects a growing contribution to interdisciplinary urban and environmental research. He has published in peer-reviewed international journals, including a study in Advances in Space Research examining the relationship between carbon concentration, urban vegetation, and climate implications using Google Earth Engine-based analysis. His research portfolio also includes conference papers on machine learning applications in LULC classification and book chapters addressing sustainability and development planning at national and regional scales. In addition, he has multiple manuscripts under review in reputable journals, covering topics such as urban heat stress, flood-related loss and damage, women's empowerment and climate resilience, and urban growth management. He also serves as a reviewer for international journals, contributing to the academic community through peer evaluation.

Methodologically, his work integrates spatial modelling, machine learning, network analysis, and geostatistical techniques to generate evidence for urban planning and climate adaptation strategies. His ongoing graduate research focuses on flood inundation modelling and data-driven decision-making for emergency response, further strengthening his expertise in hydrological modelling and risk assessment.

Alongside his academic contributions, Md. Zahid Hasan is currently working as a Junior GIS Expert in the Climate Change Adapted Urban Development Programme (CCAUD-II) implemented by HYDROPLAN Ingenieur-Gesellschaft mbH. His professional work complements his research by enabling the practical application of geospatial analysis in climate risk mapping, database development, and urban resilience planning. Previously, he held research-focused roles at the Bangladesh Institute of Planners and contributed to applied research projects with the International Union for Conservation of Nature, further bridging the gap between academic research and real-world urban policy and planning practice.
