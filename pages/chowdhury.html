Biography

Radwan Rahman Chowdhury is one of the key researchers at GeoScape Lab. He completed his Bachelor of Urban & Regional Planning from Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology. His research and professional work focus on land use and land cover mapping, spatial analysis, climate vulnerability, environmental planning, and geospatial data management.

He previously worked as a Research Consultant at CEGIS, where he contributed to national-level land use mapping projects using high-resolution satellite imagery and remote sensing techniques. His work involved managing spatial databases, maintaining GIS geodatabases, conducting land use classification, processing multi-temporal satellite imagery, and ensuring data quality through validation and topology correction workflows. He also coordinated spatial data compilation among team members, reviewed GIS outputs, and supported the preparation of technical maps, metadata, and project documentation.

Alongside geospatial analysis, Chowdhury has experience in field-based research and data collection activities, including community surveys, GPS-based data collection, and stakeholder consultations. He has worked with multidisciplinary teams on projects related to food security, groundwater sustainability, climate vulnerability, and agricultural land management in Bangladesh.

Chowdhury has experience working with WorldView-3, Sentinel-2, Landsat, and KOMPSAT imagery, as well as GIS platforms including ArcGIS and QGIS. His research interests lie in integrating remote sensing, GIS, spatial databases, and field data to support evidence-based planning, environmental research, and resource management.
