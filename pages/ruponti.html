Biography

Farhana Rabbi Ruponti is a development professional and researcher specialising in climate change adaptation, disaster risk reduction (DRR), and locally led adaptation approaches in Bangladesh. She is currently working as Assistant Project Officer – DRR & CCA at the Climate Change Adaptation and Disaster Resilience Programme of Islamic Relief Bangladesh. Her work focuses on community-based adaptation, climate-resilient planning, disaster management, and inclusive resilience building for vulnerable communities. Previously, she worked with CARE Bangladesh, contributing to community preparedness, adaptation planning, and research activities.

She completed her Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Disaster Management and Resilience from Bangladesh University of Professionals. She is currently pursuing her MSc as a COLOCAL scholar at Independent University, Bangladesh. Her research interests include climate change adaptation, gender and resilience, disaster preparedness, and locally led adaptation.
