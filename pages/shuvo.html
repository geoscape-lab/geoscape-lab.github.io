Biography

Ragib Mahmood Shuvo is a Co-Director and founding member of GeoScape Lab, and currently serves as a Lecturer in the Department of Environmental Science and Management at Independent University, Bangladesh. His research integrates climate science, geospatial analytics, remote sensing, and machine learning to address complex environmental and socio-ecological challenges in climate-vulnerable regions.

His work primarily focuses on climate change impacts, disaster risk assessment, groundwater sustainability, drought and desertification, salinity intrusion, flood modelling, urban climate dynamics, and environmental resource management. He has extensive experience applying GIS, Google Earth Engine, GeoAI, and statistical modelling techniques for spatiotemporal environmental analysis and decision-support systems. His research particularly emphasises the use of remote sensing and machine learning approaches for groundwater assessment, land-use change analysis, climate adaptation planning, and sustainability-oriented policy development.

Ragib has authored and co-authored several peer-reviewed publications in internationally recognised journals, including Applied Water Science, Water, Advances in Space Research, Energy Strategy Reviews, and Remote Sensing Applications: Society and Environment. His recent studies investigate groundwater sustainability in semi-arid Bangladesh, urban carbon imbalance and climate implications, geospatially informed water pricing systems, and machine learning-based land surface temperature analysis.

His ongoing MSc research focuses on delineating freshwater groundwater zones within the coastal aquifers of Bangladesh through integrated remote sensing and machine learning approaches. He is also actively engaged in interdisciplinary research on climate resilience, environmental governance, ecosystem service valuation, and sustainable water resource management.

Alongside his research activities, Ragib has contributed to several national-scale planning and environmental projects through positions at Bangladesh Space Research and Remote Sensing Organization, Bangladesh University Urban Lab, Bangladesh Institute of Planners, and BRAC James P Grant School of Public Health.
