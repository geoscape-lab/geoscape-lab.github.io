Biography

Sumaia Kashem is a Co-Director and co-founder of GeoScape Lab, and a doctoral researcher at Monash University, Australia. Her PhD, Behind the Algorithm: Investigating Climate Disinformation Networks in Australia, develops an integrated model of how climate disinformation operates in Australia and shapes policy. Her wider research sits at the intersection of environmental sustainability and planning, encompassing environmental resource management and economics, climate risk, adaptation and governance, integrated water resource management and water security, GIS and remote sensing, and the transport-environment nexus.

Sumaia holds an MSc in Water Resources Development from the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) and a Bachelor of Urban and Regional Planning from Rajshahi University of Engineering and Technology (RUET). Earlier in her career, she was awarded an IDRC-funded fellowship under the South Asian Water (SAWA) Leadership Program on Climate Change, and has held research and planning roles at the Bangladesh Institute of Planners, SMEC International, and DDCL.

She co-founded GeoScape as an interdisciplinary research collective, and has contributed to community-engaged work including Oxfam GB's Transboundary Rivers of South Asia (TROSA) program.
