Biography

Prem Kumer Ghosh is an Assistant Engineer at Gas Transmission Company Limited (GTCL), Bangladesh, where he is engaged in GIS-based pipeline mapping, riverbank assessment, soil investigation, and environmental management planning for national gas transmission infrastructure projects.

His academic and professional interests span environmental engineering, water resource management, and applied environmental economics. His research focuses on physicochemical water quality assessment, water pollution and treatment, air quality analysis, stormwater management, sustainable drainage systems, and the hydrodynamic impacts of climate change. He also specialises in the application of GIS, remote sensing, and geospatial technologies for environmental monitoring, contaminant tracking, and remediation planning. In addition, he incorporates economic and policy-oriented perspectives into environmental and water resource decision-making processes.

Prem earned a Master of Economics in Environmental Economics from the Dhaka School of Economics under the University of Dhaka, and completed his Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), where he received the BUET Technical and Compensatory Scholarship. His research publications include studies on tannery effluent impacts on the Dhaleshwari River and a Google Earth Engine-based investigation of carbon concentration disparities and their urban climate implications in Dhaka. He is proficient in Civil 3D, AutoCAD, ArcGIS, QGIS, and Python.
